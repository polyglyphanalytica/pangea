Pecunia -- The Atlas of Money

Pecunia is an interactive atlas of monetary history, tracing the evolution of money from ancient barter systems to modern digital currencies. Explore how currencies, financial innovations, and economic ideas spread across civilizations and continents over five millennia of human history.

How to Use:

Timeline: Use the slider at the bottom to travel through time, from 3000 BCE to 2025 CE. Press play to animate through history, or use arrow keys to step through the centuries. The density bar above the slider shows periods of high monetary activity.

Map: Click any gold marker to explore its history in the info panel. Scroll to zoom; click and drag to pan. Dashed lines show connections between related monetary systems. Use the zoom controls in the top-right corner.

Lenses: Switch between twelve different analytical lenses in the header to view different aspects of monetary history -- from innovation and trade to crises and regulation.

Threads: Open the menu and select Threads to follow narrative chains through monetary history, such as the evolution of coinage or the rise of digital money.

Compare: Select Compare mode to place two currencies side by side and examine their similarities and differences.

Search: Press Ctrl+K or click the search icon to find any currency, institution, or event by name.

Keyboard Shortcuts: Arrow keys (timeline), Space (play/pause), +/- (zoom), T (threads), C (compare), E (explore), Ctrl+K (search), Escape (close).

Part of The Pangea Project -- exploring the interconnected history of human civilization through interactive maps. Pecunia covers over 55 monetary entries spanning coins, paper money, banking institutions, financial crises, and digital currencies, connected by 15 direct links and 10 narrative threads.

Map data: Natural Earth via TopoJSON. Visualization: D3.js. All historical content is for educational purposes.
