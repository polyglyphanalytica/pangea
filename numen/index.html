Numen — The Atlas of Belief

Numen maps humanity's religious and spiritual traditions across 10,000 years of history. From prehistoric animism to modern pluralism, explore how beliefs emerged, spread, and influenced one another.

The name Numen derives from the Latin word for divine power or spirit — the sense of a sacred presence that pervades the world's many traditions.

Navigate the timeline to see which traditions were active in each era. Use lenses to explore different aspects: beliefs, practices, sacred texts, key figures, and more. Compare traditions side by side or trace conceptual threads across cultures.

Part of The Pangea Project. All descriptions aim for academic neutrality and respectful representation of every tradition.
